Doro Wot

Spicy Ethiopian Chicken Stew

Description

Doro Wat is a flavor-packed Ethiopian stew with tender cooked chicken in a fiery Berbere spiced sauce topped with boiled eggs

It is the national dish of Ethiopia and one of the most famous of all African dishes – it’s fabulous! You will find it in every Ethiopian restaurant and virtually anyone who is familiar with African cuisine will have heard of it.

Ingredients

  • A chicken cut into 12 pieces
  • Lemon pieces
  • Berbere spice
  • Tomato Paste
  • Butter
  • Slat
  • Boiled Eggs

Steps to make Yetsom Beyaynetu

  • Saute the Aromatics

    1. Prepare the Chicken - Season chicken with salt and pepper, and set aside.
    2. Saute - Heat a large pot over medium heat until hot, then add the spiced butter and onions. Sauté onions, frequently stirring, until they are deep brown – 7-10 minutes.
    3. Add Berbere Spice - the onions are caramelized or reach a deep brown color, add some more oil, followed by the berbere spice, garlic, and ginger
    4. Continue Stirring Stir for about 2-3 minutes for the flavors to blossom, and the mixture has a deep rich brown color. Be careful not to let it burn

  • Make the Stew

    1. Add Water - Then add about 2-3 cups of water. Add chicken, tomato paste, paprika, basil, and salt, and cook for about 30 minutes
    2. Then Eggs - in the eggs and lemon juice; thoroughly mix to ensure that the eggs are immersed in the sauce.
    3. Simmer - Continue cooking until chicken is tender, 10 minutes or more.
    4. Adjust Seasoning - sauce thickness and seasoning with water or broth, lemon, and salt according to preference.
    5. Serve warm.
